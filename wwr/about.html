WHITE RIVER RAFTING

Whitewater rafting is an exhilarating outdoor activity that uses an inflatable raft to navigate various degrees of rough water or rapids. It is more than just a sport; it is a way to connect with nature's raw power while building camaraderie with your fellow paddlers. Whether you are seeking the adrenaline-pumping challenges of Class IV rapids or a scenic float through breathtaking canyons, rafting offers an unforgettable perspective of the wild rivers that shape our world.

History

The origins of river running date back to 1811 when the first recorded attempt to navigate the Snake River in Wyoming was made. Lacking the modern equipment we use today, these early explorers used heavy wooden boats that were easily damaged. It wasn't until the 1940s, following World War II, that surplus inflatable rubber rafts became available, making the sport safer and more accessible to the public.
